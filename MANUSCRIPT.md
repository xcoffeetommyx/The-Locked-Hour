# The Locked Hour — Manuscript & Story Bible

Author credit: xCoffeeTommyx

## Format

**The Locked Hour** is a static digital horror novella / virtual book, not a game.

There are no choices, branching routes, inventory systems, puzzles, combat, or win/loss states. The reader simply turns pages through a traditional story.

The web page should behave like a polished digital book:
- Previous / Next page navigation
- Save/resume progress
- Chapter title and page progress
- Responsive mobile-first layout
- Static hosting on GitHub Pages and itch.io

## Visual Direction

The book uses page styling as part of the storytelling.

### Normal world pages
Clean, readable, modern book styling:
- Warm off-white page
- Elegant readable serif body font
- Minimal UI
- Stable margins and page numbers
- Calm, grounded atmosphere

### Old world pages
Aged, cold, old-world style:
- Weathered paper tone
- Darker page edges
- Older serif or typewriter-like accent
- Subtle CSS grain/noise
- Slightly tighter or harsher typography
- Faint winter/old church feeling
- No cheesy horror fonts

### Mixed world pages
Used when the old world and normal world begin merging:
- Normal book layout remains readable
- Old-world stains and darkened edges creep in
- Some typography shifts subtly
- Page progress or ornaments may feel unstable
- Restrained red/brown accent for Thomas's arm-time motif
- Contaminated, not chaotic

### After pages
Mostly clean again:
- Normal-world book style
- One faint old-world stain/scar remains
- Calm final tone

## Core Story Rules

- Thomas narrates the entire story in first person.
- Emily is the female deuteragonist and is present as herself.
- Thomas and Emily both remember both worlds.
- The normal world starts ordinary and grounded.
- The old world is a snowbound, older village with no cars.
- The old world contains ordinary animals and wrong/mutated animals. The threat is a corrupted ecosystem, not one single monster.
- The boarded church is a sanctuary, not a secret villain.
- The giant front lock/lever never fully locks. It always stops about one inch short and is treated as "good enough."
- Regular nightly locking only survives the cycle. It does not break the Locked Hour.
- Thomas's watch disappears in the old world. The time appears as part of his forearm and keeps skipping. It is not a code or combination.
- The young girl is a warning/last rememberer, not a villain. Her warning is initially incomprehensible.
- The warning eventually resolves into: **"After the hour is locked... don't let anyone in."**
- Emily is left outside after the lock and returns changed because Thomas lets her in after the hour is locked.
- By the end, everyone in the church has vanished except Thomas and Emily.
- Thomas and Emily complete the final inch of the lock together.
- The ending resolves the emotional story but does not overexplain the full supernatural mythology.

## Character Bible

### Thomas
- First-person narrator.
- Grounded, practical, observant.
- Does not narrate like a horror writer; he describes impossible things like a normal person trying to make sense of them.
- Watches out for Emily as a natural habit.
- Recurring emotional thread: asks Emily to text him when she gets home.

### Emily
- Close friend/coworker of Thomas.
- Natural, playful dialogue with Thomas.
- Observant and pattern-focused.
- Notices things before Thomas sometimes.
- Notices Thomas's watch is gone before he says anything, but waits because she hopes she is wrong.
- Sees/hears the young girl more clearly before Thomas in later chapters.
- After being let in after the lock, she is still Emily but subtly wrong: pauses too long, stares at Thomas's arm, smiles a fraction too long, hears another voice.

### Samuel
- Older survivor in the church.
- Not a mystical lore-keeper.
- Knows the rules because he has survived them, but does not know why the rules exist.
- Key line: **"I know the rules. I don't know why they're the rules."**
- Makes impossible decisions because he has had to do so many times.
- Key line after closing the door on someone outside: **"You never get used to it."**

### The Young Girl
- First encountered by Thomas and Emily while walking home after work.
- Speaks to both, but neither can understand her.
- Appears later in the old world and eventually inside/near the church.
- She has forgotten her own name.
- Her role is to warn, not to trap.
- Final clear warning: **"After the hour is locked... don't let anyone in."**
- Final line before disappearing: **"Thank you."**

# Chapter 1 — The Girl on the Walk Home

**World:** normal

## Page 1

My name is Thomas.

For a long time, I convinced myself none of this happened.

Then I noticed the scar on my arm was still there.

This is everything I remember.

## Page 2

It started after work.

Nothing about that evening felt important at first.

Emily and I had just finished an eight-hour shift, the kind that somehow found a way to feel longer than it was.

The sky had already gone dark by the time we left.

Winter always did that.

You'd get off work and somehow the day was already over.

## Page 3

Emily

"Eight hours," Emily said.

Thomas

"Yep."

Emily

"Felt like twelve."

Thomas

"Only because you spent six of them waiting to clock out."

She looked at me like I had personally offended her.

Emily

"I was working."

Thomas

"Sure you were."

She bumped my shoulder with hers.

Emily

"You know what? Next shift, you can deal with all the customers."

Thomas

"I already do."

Emily

"Exactly. You're getting practice."

## Page 4

We walked the same way we usually did.

Past the closed storefronts.

Past the parking lot lights.

Past the little stretch of sidewalk where the street got quieter before we split off toward home.

Neither of us was in a hurry.

That was normal too.

Sometimes the walk after work felt like the only part of the day that belonged to us.

## Page 5

Thomas

"You doing anything tomorrow?" I asked.

Emily

"Sleeping," Emily said.

Thomas

"That's not a plan."

Emily

"It's absolutely a plan."

Thomas

"Until noon?"

Emily

"At least."

Thomas

"I don't think I've ever seen you awake before lunch on your day off."

Emily

"That's because you're always texting me before lunch."

Thomas

"Fair."

## Page 6

We were still laughing when Emily slowed down.

Not stopped.

Slowed.

I followed her eyes.

A little girl stood ahead of us on the sidewalk.

She was alone.

No adult nearby.

No phone in her hand.

No backpack.

She just stood beneath the streetlight, facing us.

## Page 7

At first, I thought she was lost.

Then we got closer.

Her clothes were wrong.

Not costume wrong.

Older than that.

A plain dress. Dark shoes. Hair hanging loose around her face.

She looked cold, but she wasn't shivering.

Emily's voice softened.

Emily

"Sweetie... are you okay?"

The girl looked at her.

Then at me.

## Page 8

The girl opened her mouth.

She spoke.

Or tried to.

I heard the sound of a voice, but none of the words.

It wasn't another language.

It wasn't static.

It was like pieces of the sentence had been removed before they reached us.

I looked at Emily.

Thomas

"Did you understand that?"

She shook her head.

Emily

"No."

## Page 9

The girl tried again.

Her expression changed.

More urgent.

More afraid.

She pointed at us.

Then behind us.

Emily turned first.

I turned with her.

The sidewalk behind us was empty.

Just parked cars, streetlights, and the road shining faintly from old rain.

Nothing else.

## Page 10

When we looked back, the girl was gone.

Not running.

Not hiding.

Gone.

Emily and I stood there longer than we should have.

I checked between the buildings.

Under the streetlight.

Down the sidewalk.

There was nowhere she could have gone that quickly.

Emily finally spoke.

Emily

"That was weird."

## Page 11

Thomas

"Maybe she ran," I said.

Emily looked at me.

Emily

"That fast?"

I shrugged.

It was a bad explanation.

We both knew it.

Thomas

"Maybe her parents were close by."

Emily

"Thomas."

Thomas

"What?"

Emily

"Did that look like a kid messing around to you?"

I didn't answer.

Because no.

It didn't.

## Page 12

We kept walking.

The rest of the way felt different.

Not dangerous exactly.

Just quieter.

Like the street had noticed us noticing it.

When we reached the place where we normally split up, I stopped.

Emily looked over.

Emily

"What?"

Thomas

"Text me when you get home."

She smiled.

Emily

"I always do."

Thomas

"I know."

Emily

"Then why do you keep saying it?"

I shrugged.

Thomas

"Habit."

## Page 13

She started walking backward for a few steps.

Emily

"Night, Thomas."

Thomas

"Night, Emily."

She turned the corner.

I waited until I couldn't see her anymore.

Then I went home.

Later, my phone buzzed.

Emily.

Still thinking about that little girl.

I typed back:

Me too.

## Page 14

A few seconds later, another message came through.

She was trying to tell us something.

I stared at that sentence for a long time.

Then I wrote:

We'll figure it out tomorrow.

Emily replied:

Goodnight, Thomas.

I sent:

Night, Emily.

Then I put my phone on the nightstand, turned off the light, and closed my eyes.

I expected to wake up in my bed.

I woke up freezing.

# Chapter 2 — Snow Without Roads

**World:** old

## Page 1

I woke up freezing.

Not the kind of cold that makes you pull the blanket closer.

The kind that hurts.

The air burned every time I breathed. My fingers felt stiff before I even opened my eyes.

For a second I thought the heater had gone out.

Then I reached for the blanket.

There wasn't one.

My hand sank into snow.

## Page 2

I sat up too quickly.

White.

Everywhere.

Snow drifted across a road that wasn't really a road anymore. It was frozen dirt with wagon ruts buried beneath fresh snowfall.

I turned slowly, trying to make sense of where I was.

There were no cars.

No streetlights.

No sidewalks.

Just weathered wooden buildings with steep roofs carrying too much snow, their windows glowing faintly with lantern light.

The wind carried the smell of smoke.

And something else.

Wet animals.

## Page 3

My first thought wasn't that I had traveled somewhere.

It was that I was dreaming.

I laughed once under my breath.

It sounded nervous.

Thomas

"This is a dream."

Saying it out loud helped for exactly three seconds.

The cold didn't feel like a dream.

When I breathed in, my lungs hurt.

When I stood up, snow slipped into my shoes.

Dreams weren't supposed to remember details like that.

## Page 4

Something moved behind me.

I spun around so fast I nearly slipped.

Emily

"Thomas!"

Emily.

She was twenty feet away, standing in the middle of the road.

I don't think I'd ever been happier to see another person.

She ran toward me, stopping just short.

"You see this too?"

I stared at her.

Thomas

"You remember?"

Her expression changed immediately.

Emily

"So you remember."

Neither of us smiled.

## Page 5

For several long seconds we simply stood there.

Neither of us asked the obvious question.

How?

Instead, Emily looked around.

Emily

"This isn't where we were," Emily said.

Thomas

"No."

Emily

"It doesn't even..."

She turned in a slow circle.

"...make sense."

Thomas

"It has to be a dream."

She looked back at me.

Emily

"If it's a dream..."

"...why are you here too?"

I didn't have an answer.

## Page 6

A church bell echoed somewhere in the distance.

One slow strike.

Then silence.

Emily flinched.

Emily

"You heard that?"

Thomas

"Yeah."

Emily

"It sounded..."

She stopped.

Thomas

"What?"

Emily

"I don't know."

We listened.

Nothing.

Just the wind.

Then somewhere much farther away...

A wolf howled.

Another answered.

Then another.

## Page 7

Emily

"We should move," Emily said quietly.

Thomas

"Toward the bell?"

Emily

"It's the only thing that sounds... human."

We started walking.

The village felt abandoned.

Doors were shut tight.

Curtains covered every window.

No voices.

No children.

No smoke rising from chimneys except one or two in the distance.

Even the snow looked untouched.

Until I looked down.

Footprints.

Hundreds of them.

All leading the same direction.

Toward the bell.

## Page 8

As we followed the tracks, something caught Emily's attention.

Emily

"Thomas."

Thomas

"What?"

She pointed at my wrist.

Emily

"You still wearing that watch?" she asked.

I looked down.

Thomas

"Yeah."

She frowned.

Emily

"Good."

Thomas

"Why?"

Emily

"I don't know."

She looked away almost immediately, embarrassed.

"I just... wanted to make sure."

I laughed.

Thomas

"That's oddly specific."

She forced a smile.

Emily

"I know."

Neither of us understood why she'd asked.

## Page 9

The village opened into a wider square.

Beyond it stood the largest building I'd ever seen made entirely of dark timber.

A church.

Or at least something built to be one.

The windows had been boarded over from the inside.

Heavy iron straps crossed the massive front doors.

Fixed to those doors was the strangest lock I'd ever seen.

Not a padlock.

Not a chain.

A huge iron mechanism with a long horizontal lever that looked as though it took all of someone's strength just to move it.

It wasn't decoration.

It had been built because someone expected to use it.

Often.

## Page 10

Before either of us could get closer—

A Survivor

"Run!"

The voice came from somewhere to our left.

Three people emerged from between the buildings.

Their clothes looked as old as everything else around us.

One of them grabbed my arm.

Another reached for Emily.

"Don't just stand there!"

Thomas

"What's happening?" I shouted.

A Survivor

"No time!"

They practically dragged us across the square.

Behind us, something barked.

Not a dog.

Something heavier.

More voices shouted from inside the church.

The great doors began to open.

Only a crack.

Just enough for people to slip through.

"Move!"

We crossed the threshold.

The doors slammed shut behind us.

Outside...

The wolves began to gather.

# Chapter 3 — The Lock That Wouldn't Close

**World:** old

## Page 1

The doors slammed shut behind us with a force that shook the floor beneath my boots.

For a moment, no one moved.

The only sound was heavy breathing.

Mine.

Emily's.

Everyone else's.

Then someone shouted.

A Voice

"Bar it!"

The room came alive.

## Page 2

People moved with the speed of repetition.

Not panic.

Practice.

Men and women rushed toward the enormous iron lever fixed across the inside of the church doors.

No one argued.

No one asked questions.

It was obvious they'd done this before.

Many times.

## Page 3

Emily

"Thomas!"

Emily grabbed my sleeve.

"They're telling us to help."

One of the older men waved us over.

The Old Man

"If you're standing, you're pushing."

I didn't even think about it.

I put both hands on the freezing iron beside everyone else.

It felt smooth from years of use.

## Page 4

A Voice

"Ready."

Nobody counted.

Everyone simply pushed together.

The lever groaned.

Metal scraped against metal with a sound so deep I could feel it through my hands.

It moved.

Slowly.

Painfully.

Halfway.

Three quarters.

Almost there.

Then...

It stopped.

## Page 5

Everyone kept pushing.

Someone grunted.

Another man braced his shoulder against the iron.

I leaned my full weight into it.

Nothing.

It refused to move another inch.

The old man finally let go.

The Old Man

"That's enough."

I looked at him.

Thomas

"...Enough?"

He nodded.

The Old Man

"It won't go any farther."

## Page 6

I looked past him.

The lever hadn't reached the last notch.

It was close.

Painfully close.

Maybe an inch away.

Maybe less.

It looked unfinished.

Thomas

"Shouldn't it lock all the way?"

No one answered immediately.

Finally, a woman near the front said quietly,

A Woman

"It hasn't... in a very long time."

## Page 7

I stared at the gap.

My brain hated it.

Everything about it felt wrong.

Doors were either locked...

...or they weren't.

Emily stepped beside me.

Emily

"It really won't move?" Emily asked.

The old man shook his head.

The Old Man

"It never does."

Emily

"You've tried?"

A tired smile crossed his face.

The Old Man

"Every night."

## Page 8

Every night.

The words landed harder than they should have.

Every night.

Not once.

Not occasionally.

Every night.

Emily looked at me.

I could tell she was thinking the same thing I was.

How many nights had these people been doing this?

## Page 9

Only then did I really look around.

The church was enormous.

Rows of old wooden pews stretched into darkness.

Blankets covered entire families sleeping against the walls.

Lanterns burned with weak yellow flames.

Everything smelled of old wood, smoke, damp wool...

...and cold.

It wasn't a shelter someone had found.

It looked like people had been living here.

## Page 10

Something struck the outside wall.

Not hard.

Just...

one solid thud.

Everyone froze.

A little boy sitting beside his mother stopped talking.

Nobody looked surprised.

They only listened.

Another sound.

Scratching.

Slow.

Moving around the outside of the church.

## Page 11

Emily leaned closer.

Emily

"Thomas..."

Thomas

"I hear it," I said.

Emily

"It sounds like..."

She stopped.

The scratching became footsteps.

More than one set.

Circling.

The old man quietly picked up a lantern.

The Old Man

"They're early tonight."

No one asked who "they" were.

## Page 12

I walked toward one of the boarded windows.

A hand caught my arm before I reached it.

The Old Man

"Don't."

The old man again.

Thomas

"Why?"

He looked at the boards.

The Old Man

"Sometimes..."

He hesitated.

"...they know when you're looking."

I waited for him to smile.

He didn't.

## Page 13

The scratching stopped.

Silence.

Complete silence.

Even the wind outside seemed to disappear.

Then...

A long, low howl rolled through the walls.

Another answered.

Closer.

Then another.

Not just wolves.

Something deeper.

Larger.

Wrong somehow.

The howls echoed across the snow until I couldn't tell how many surrounded us.

## Page 14

Emily spoke so quietly I almost didn't hear her.

Emily

"I don't think those are all wolves."

Before I could answer...

Something heavy brushed against the outside of the church.

The wall creaked.

Dust drifted from the ceiling.

Nobody screamed.

Nobody ran.

The people simply waited.

Like this was the most ordinary part of their evening.

I looked back at the unfinished lock.

Still one inch short.

Still...

good enough.

# Chapter 4 — Morning With Snow Still on My Shoes

**World:** normal

## Page 1

I woke up in my own bed.

For one wonderful second...

...everything was normal.

My room.

My ceiling.

The quiet hum of the air conditioner.

Morning sunlight slipping through the blinds.

I laughed.

Not because anything was funny.

Because I had never been so happy to wake up.

Thomas

"It was just a dream."

I believed it.

For about five seconds.

## Page 2

The cold hadn't left.

My room wasn't cold.

I was.

It sat somewhere beneath my skin.

Like I had stood outside too long and my body hadn't realized it was warm again.

I rubbed my hands together.

They still felt stiff.

Almost numb.

I shook my head.

Thomas

"You're overthinking it."

That sounded reasonable.

Until I stood up.

## Page 3

My shoes were beside the bedroom door.

I don't know why I looked at them.

Maybe because something felt...

off.

I picked one up.

The tread was packed with dried mud.

Not fresh.

Not soaking wet.

Dry.

Cracked.

Like it had been there for hours.

I stared at it for a long time.

Then I laughed again.

Much quieter this time.

## Page 4

Thomas

"No."

I checked the rest of the room.

Nothing.

The carpet was clean.

The floor was dry.

No footprints.

No snow.

Just...

mud in my shoes.

I tried to remember yesterday.

Work.

Walking home with Emily.

The little girl.

Then...

Nothing.

Until the snow.

## Page 5

I looked at my watch.

Still on my wrist.

Exactly where it belonged.

The second hand moved.

Steady.

Normal.

No skipping.

No missing time.

I don't think I've ever been happier to see an ordinary watch.

I checked the date.

The day.

Everything looked right.

I told myself that settled it.

It didn't.

## Page 6

My phone buzzed.

Emily.

Not a text.

A phone call.

I answered immediately.

Neither of us spoke.

Finally...

Emily

"You remember."

It wasn't a question.

Thomas

"No," I said automatically.

A pause.

Then I sighed.

"...Yeah."

## Page 7

Thomas

"You?"

Emily

"I remember all of it," Emily said.

Thomas

"The church?"

Emily

"Yes."

Thomas

"The wolves?"

Another pause.

Emily

"...Yes."

Thomas

"The people?"

Emily

"I remember them too."

Neither of us said the word dream.

Not once.

## Page 8

Thomas

"I checked my shoes," I said.

Emily was quiet.

Emily

"So did I."

My stomach dropped.

Thomas

"There was mud."

Emily

"There was mud."

Neither of us laughed.

We couldn't.

It wasn't funny anymore.

## Page 9

Thomas

"I keep trying to remember how we got home," I said.

Emily

"I know."

Thomas

"I can't."

Emily

"I can't either."

Silence.

Then Emily asked something that hadn't occurred to me.

"Do you remember waking up there?"

I frowned.

Thomas

"Yeah."

Emily

"No."

She interrupted gently.

"I mean...

"Do you remember falling asleep here?"

I opened my mouth.

Nothing came out.

I couldn't remember.

## Page 10

The realization settled over both of us at the same time.

I remembered going to bed.

I remembered snow.

Everything between those two moments...

was gone.

No drifting off.

No dreaming.

No transition.

One moment I was in my room.

The next...

I was freezing in the snow.

## Page 11

Emily

"What if..." Emily started.

She stopped herself.

Thomas

"What?"

Emily

"It sounds stupid."

Thomas

"Try me."

Another long pause.

Emily

"What if..."

She took a breath.

"...what if we weren't dreaming?"

I looked out my bedroom window.

Cars.

Birds.

Morning traffic.

Everything looked perfectly ordinary.

Thomas

"It had to be a dream."

Emily

"It doesn't feel like one."

Thomas

"No."

Emily

"It doesn't."

## Page 12

Emily

"We should stay awake tonight," Emily said.

I laughed.

A tired laugh.

"Seriously," she said.

Thomas

"I'm serious too," I told her.

"We don't know that's what caused it."

Emily

"We don't know it didn't."

She had a point.

Neither of us liked it.

## Page 13

Thomas

"I'll make coffee," I said.

Emily

"I already am."

Thomas

"I'll keep texting you."

Emily

"I know."

Thomas

"If one of us starts getting tired..."

Emily

"We call," Emily said.

Thomas

"No matter how late."

Emily

"No matter how late."

It sounded ridiculous.

Like children making rules against monsters.

Neither of us smiled.

## Page 14

Before we hung up, Emily said my name.

Emily

"Thomas."

Thomas

"Yeah?"

Emily

"If we do go back..."

I waited.

"...don't let me get separated from you."

I swallowed.

Thomas

"I won't."

Another pause.

Then she quietly said,

Emily

"I believe you."

The call ended.

I looked down at the dried mud still caught in my shoe.

Outside, the morning sun was bright enough to make me squint.

For the first time in my life...

I wasn't looking forward to going to bed.

# Chapter 5 — Fighting Sleep

**World:** normal

## Page 1

Work felt unreal the next day.

Not bad.

Not busy.

Just...

thin.

Like the world had become a set someone had forgotten to finish building.

Customers talked.

Registers beeped.

Doors opened and closed.

Everything sounded normal.

That was the worst part.

Normal had started to feel suspicious.

## Page 2

I kept looking at people too long.

An old man buying batteries had Samuel's eyes.

A woman at the end of an aisle laughed like someone I had heard inside the church.

A child ran past me with a wooden toy in his hand.

Not the same toy.

Not even close.

But close enough that I had to look away.

## Page 3

Emily found me near the back after lunch.

She didn't ask if I was okay.

That was one of the things I liked about her.

She already knew the answer.

Emily

"You look terrible," she said.

Thomas

"Good to see you too."

Emily

"I mean worse than usual."

Thomas

"That helps."

Emily crossed her arms.

Emily

"You sleep?"

I looked at her.

Thomas

"Did you?"

Emily

"No."

Thomas

"Then no."

## Page 4

We agreed to meet at my place after work.

Not because either of us had a plan.

Because doing nothing felt worse.

Emily showed up with a notebook, two energy drinks, and the expression of someone who had decided fear was less useful than preparation.

I opened the door.

She held up the drinks.

Emily

"Supplies."

Thomas

"That's your survival plan?"

Emily

"It's a start."

## Page 5

We spread everything across my kitchen table.

Phones.

Chargers.

Coffee.

Her notebook.

A half-finished map I had printed from the internet.

Emily had written down everything she remembered.

Not just the big things.

Everything.

The church doors.

The lever.

The old man.

The woman with the hammer.

The wolves.

The loose board near the window.

## Page 6

Thomas

"You wrote all this?"

Emily shrugged.

Emily

"I didn't want to forget."

I flipped through the pages.

Her handwriting was messier than usual, but careful.

Determined.

"I counted twenty-three people," she said.

Thomas

"I would've guessed thirty."

Emily

"There were twenty-three."

Thomas

"You sure?"

She pointed at the notebook.

Emily

"I counted twice."

## Page 7

I sat back.

Thomas

"Of course you did."

Emily

"What does that mean?"

Thomas

"Nothing."

Emily

"Thomas."

Thomas

"It means you would count people in a nightmare."

Emily looked offended for half a second.

Then smiled.

Emily

"Someone has to be useful."

Thomas

"I was useful."

Emily

"You pushed a door."

Thomas

"A very important door."

Emily

"Badly."

Thomas

"It was already broken."

## Page 8

The joke died faster than either of us expected.

I drew the lever from memory.

A rough sketch.

Too simple.

Too wrong.

Still...

Looking at it made my stomach tighten.

Emily leaned over the table.

Emily

"It never closed."

Thomas

"I know."

Emily

"No."

She tapped the drawing.

"It wasn't broken."

I looked at her.

Thomas

"How do you know?"

Emily

"Because everyone acted like that was normal."

## Page 9

That bothered me more than I wanted to admit.

The lever stopping short should have terrified them.

Instead, they treated it like weather.

The time hadn't skipped.

Not once.

I never looked back.

Neither did Emily.

We never saw the empty sidewalk behind us.

Or the little footprints in the morning dew.

They faded with the sunlight.

As though they had never been there at all.

# After

**World:** after

## Page 1

People ask what dreams are.

Memories.

Fears.

Pieces of things we've forgotten.

Maybe they're right.

Maybe they're not.

I don't know anymore.

Some questions don't need answers.

Only endings.

## Page 2

Emily and I never tried to explain what happened.

Not to each other.

Not to anyone else.

We didn't need to.

Some experiences become smaller when you force them into words.

This wasn't one of them.

It simply...

became part of us.

## Page 3

The scar faded over the next few weeks.

Slowly.

Until only I knew where to look.

My watch never skipped again.

I still caught myself checking my arm sometimes.

Old habits don't disappear overnight.

Emily would notice.

She never laughed.

She'd just smile and quietly say,

Emily

"It's still on your wrist."

Then I'd look down.

And she'd be right.

Every time.

## Page 4

We kept walking home together after work.

Sometimes we talked the entire way.

Sometimes we didn't.

Silence never felt awkward anymore.

After everything we'd seen...

Quiet had become something precious.

Winter came.

Then spring.

Then another summer.

Life kept moving.

Like it always does.

## Page 5

Every now and then...

I'd catch myself listening.

Not for voices.

Not for bells.

Just...

listening.

The world sounds different after you've heard it go completely silent.

Emily admitted she did the same.

Neither of us ever said why.

We didn't have to.

## Page 6

One evening, almost a year later, we took the long way home.

The sun was beginning to set.

The same warm orange light that had filled the sky the day everything started.

We reached the corner where we'd first seen the little girl.

Without saying a word...

We both stopped walking.

The sidewalk looked ordinary.

Cars passed.

People laughed.

A dog barked somewhere across the street.

Nothing was unusual.

Emily looked at me.

Emily

"You felt it too?"

I nodded.

Thomas

"Yeah."

She smiled.

Emily

"I thought so."

We stood there another minute.

Then we kept walking.

## Page 7

Emily

"Thomas?"

I looked over.

Thomas

"Yeah?" I said.

She laughed softly.

Emily

"Text me when you get home."

I laughed too.

Thomas

"I will."

We both knew neither of us lived very far away.

We both knew it wasn't necessary.

But some promises...

Mean more after you've almost broken them forever.

## Page 8

I watched her disappear around the corner.

Then I checked my watch.

The second hand moved.

Steady.

Perfectly ordinary.

I smiled.

I turned toward home.

And I kept walking.

Behind me...

The evening breeze stirred a few fallen leaves across the sidewalk.

For the briefest moment...

They sounded almost like tiny footsteps.

I didn't turn around.

Neither of us would have.

Some doors are meant to stay closed.

Some hours are meant to stay locked.

And some stories...

Are finally allowed to end.

**The End.**
